Chavi Samajik Sanstha has been continuously working for sustainable development, increase in means of livelihood, awareness and advanced agriculture in rural areas since 2011.

In about 54 villages in the tribal dominated area of Mhow block in Indore district, we worked as a partner of Nagarth Charitable Trust under the Integrated Watershed Management Scheme of the Central Government. 17 watershed committees have been established, each consisting of 7 registered members (approx. 45% women). Currently, a total of 119 committee members are actively involved in implementing the project.

Past Projects

Nagarth Charitable Trust — Watershed Management

We began with participatory rural assessment (PRA), baseline surveys, and exposure visits for capacity development. Micro watershed committees were formed in all villages with objectives of water requirement planning, livelihoods, crop productivity, and advanced agriculture.

Cleanup Drives with Indore Municipal Corporation

Organized drives with ~150 university students for restoration of heritage water bodies:

  • G.T.S. Stepwell, Zone No. 4
  • Kamla Nehru Stepwell, Zone No. 1
  • Champa Stepwell, Zone No. 15

Community Shramdan for Water Conservation

A Shramdan was organized by Chavi Samajik Sanstha at Maya Khedi Pond and Champa Bawdi in the Lalbagh area. The event was graced by the Commissioner of Indore Municipal Corporation, .

Rainwater Harvesting Awareness

In collaboration with Indore Municipal Corporation, technical guidance and awareness for installation of rainwater harvesting systems across 22 zones in households, societies, industrial & commercial establishments.

Current Projects

Economic & Financial Literacy Program

In collaboration with Swadhaar Foundation, supported by HSBC Bank:

  • 1200 trained, 160 linked to financial products
  • Money management, savings accounts, RD/FD, postal schemes
  • Government schemes: insurance, pensions, Sukanya Samriddhi
  • Digital banking (UPI, mobile banking)

Saksham Anganwadis Project

Transformation of 196 Anganwadis into self-reliant centers by installing Rainwater Harvesting systems. Anganwadi workers trained on water management & sustainability, ensuring water security, environmental awareness, and community empowerment.

Nukkad Natak on different topics
